When it comes to dogs, there are lots of different diets to choose from and knowing what to feed your furry friend can be a minefield. To help you out, weâ€™ve outlined the different types of diets to help you choose the perfect food for your pooch!

Different Varietiesof Dog food at our service incude;

  • Kibble/Dry

    • Dry dog food is the most cost-effective sort of commercial dog food, which is why many owners pick it for their pets. It also has a long shelf life and does not require refrigeration. Chewing crunchy dry food can also assist to keep your dog's teeth healthy, since it helps to decrease tartar buildup. When picking a dry food, carefully check the contents and choose a brand that uses wholesome food as its principal element.

  • Canned

    • Canned, or wet, food is popular with most dogs; it has a long shelf life and is easy to find in any store, but it can be costly. Although some owners believe it is well worth the money, not every type of commercial canned food contains the protein that your dog need. The main question is how much protein is digested. Indigestible protein is useless to your dog since it passes through his system without being broken down into absorbable nutrients.In addition, most canned foods contain roughly 75% water. Because more water content means less nutrient content, your dog will need to eat more food to acquire the nutritional value his body requires. If you decide to feed your dog canned food, there are a few things to consider.

  • Semi-Moist

    • Semi-moist foods are commercial dog foods styled like pork chops, burgers, or other meaty delicacies. These diets are the least nutritious of all dog foods, and they contain a lot of artificial tastes and colors. They can be given to your dog as a treat on occasion, but they should not be considered a complete diet because they lack the nourishment that your dog requires.

  • Raw

    • Raw meat, preferably with some bones (never cooked bones, only raw) and organs mixed throughout, is the foundation of a raw diet. Bones are a natural supply of phosphate and calcium. Many dogs benefit from this type of diet because they have short intestinal tracts and powerful stomach acids, which make it simple for them to eat and digest raw food. Consult your veterinarian about the benefits and hazards of switching your dog to a raw diet.
